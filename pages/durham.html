Durham

We love living in Durham! Here are some fun things to do and places to eat to try out while you're visting.

Restaurants

Toast
Our first date was at Toast, and we still love going there! Great hot sandwiches with real Italian panini ingredients.
Pompieri Pizza
Fun pizza shack in an old firehouse, serving one-size-fits-all Neapolitan pizzas. They also serve Lambrusco (delicious red bubbly wine we love) by the glass or bottle.
Gregoria's Cuban Steakhouse
We've actually never ordered a steak here because the roast pork is so good. They also make create plantains, either roasted (maduros), fried once (plaintain chips), or fried twice (tostones).

Sweets

Locopops
Great for summer: Mexican paletas, water-based and cream-based popsicles. They do some cool things with mint and fruit.
The Parlour
Best ice cream in town (and we've tried quite a few).

Bars

Fullsteam Brewery
The friendliest place in down. Good beer and an open environment that doesn't mean you've got a waiter checking on you every 5 minutes. Kid-friendly and pet-friendly, and a great place to sit and relax.
Alley Twenty-Six
This is where we go to dress up and splurge on old-timey cocktails like gin fizzes.

Family stuff

Museum of Life and Science
Great museum for all ages, with indoor exhibits on space, weather, geology and biology, and a huge outdoor area with lemurs, bears, and wolves. (Hint: great place to let kids run around.) Runs around $10 for kids and $14-15 for adults.
Sarah P. Duke Gardens
Gardens maintained by Duke, including local plants, exotic plants, a lake that attracts waterfowl, and some other interesting sights. Free admission; parking for a couple of bucks.
