Durham

We love living in Durham. Here are our favorite spots for when you visit.

Restaurants

Toast Paninoteca
Our first date was at Toast, and it's still our favorite. The real deal when it comes to paninis.
Pompieri Pizza
Fun pizza place in Durham's historic Firehouse #1, serving one-size-fits-all Neapolitan pizzas and Lambrusco by the glass or bottle.
Elmo's Diner
Family-friendly diner that serves breakfast all day.
Gregoria's Cuban Steakhouse
We've actually never ordered a steak here because the roast pork is so good. They also make great plantains, either roasted (maduros), fried once (plaintain chips), or fried twice (tostones).
Taberna Tapas
We go in here for a light bite or a glass of Spanish wine. Or favorite tapas are the bacon-wrapped dates and the roasted butternut squash.
Geer Street Garden
Good late-night hangout with a simple, focused Southern menu.

Sweets

Locopops
Great year-round: Mexican paletas, water-based and cream-based popsicles. Julie prefers the cream-based pops like blueberry lemon, and Tim likes the mint and fruit water-based pops.
The Parlour
Best ice cream in town (and we've tried quite a few). Open late.

Bars

Fullsteam Brewery
Good beer in a casual environment. Kid-friendly, pet-friendly, and a great place to sit and relax.
Dain's Place
Julie's neighborhood bar, a short walk from her house. A burgers-and-tater-tots kind of place.
City Beverage
Tim's neighborhood bar, with an eclectic menu, goofy retro decor, and a long list of taps.
Alley Twenty-Six
This is where we go to dress up and splurge on old-timey cocktails like gin fizzes.

Fun things to do

Museum of Life and Science
Great family-friendly museum with indoor science exhibits and a multi-acre outdoor area with a kids' train, walking paths, and animals.
Nasher Museum
Fine art museum on Duke's campus.
Letters Bookshop
Great used and new bookstore with a carefully curated collection.
Nearly New Thrift Shoppe
Best thrift shop in town (and Julie knows 'em). Run by the Duke Medical School Faculty Wives Club.
Ninth Street
Best spot to find an assortment of local shopping and restaurants (including Elmo's and Dain's).
Durham Farmer's Market
Confluence of local food, food trucks, and local art. Runs from 8-12 on Saturday morning.
The Scrap Exchange
Sells odds and ends of all kinds for craft projects.

Outdoors

Cornwallis Road Park
One of the only disc golf courses in the area. Includes a small playground.
Duke Lemur Center
Research and rehab center for lemurs. Reservations required.
Sarah P. Duke Gardens
Public gardens at Duke University, including walking paths and a lake to feed ducks. Free admission.
Eno River State Park
Hiking trails and peace and quiet within a few miles of downtown.
Occoneechee Mountain
A little farther from town; high scenic views.
